# EntraForce — Sales Pitch Script

A ~10-minute conversational pitch for prospective clients. Mixed audience (general local SMBs + South Asian-owned businesses in DFW & Chicagoland). Covers Websites, Content, Apps, and Google Business Profile management.

This is not a script to read word-for-word. It's a structure with anchor sentences. Adapt to the room.

---

## 0. Pre-call setup (2 min before)

- Pull up their existing website (or note that they don't have one).
- Pull up their Google Business Profile. Note: hours wrong? Photos old? Reviews unanswered? Categories generic?
- Search their business name + city on Google. What ranks? Yelp? A competitor? Nothing?
- You now have specific, real evidence to reference. **This is your most powerful weapon.** Don't skip it.

---

## 1. Open · 60 seconds

> "Thanks for making time. Before I tell you about EntraForce, I want to know what made you take this call. What's the thing that, if it got fixed, would matter most to your business this year?"

**Why this works:** You're letting them frame the problem. Everything after this is positioned against *their* answer, not your generic pitch.

**Listen for:**
- "We don't have a website" → lead with Starter/Standard
- "Our site is old" → lead with redesign + Care+ for ongoing
- "We're not showing up on Google" → lead with GBP management + SEO
- "We need online ordering / booking" → lead with Premium or App
- "I want to grow but I don't know where to start" → lead with discovery, multi-service

## 3. The Problem · 90 seconds

Don't lecture. Make it about them. Use what you found in pre-call.

> "Before I tell you what we'd do, can I share what I noticed when I looked you up earlier?"

Then say one or two **specific** observations:
- "Your Google listing has 41 reviews but I didn't see any responses — that's leaving traffic on the table, because Google rewards profiles that are active."
- "Your site loaded in about 6 seconds on my phone. Most people leave at 3."
- "You don't have a 'menu' link on Google — that's the #1 reason people tap a restaurant profile."
- "I couldn't find your business in the top 10 results for [their main keyword]. Your competitor [X] is ranking. We can change that."

Then zoom out:

> "Most small businesses are losing customers before those customers ever walk through the door. Not because the business is bad — because what people find online doesn't reflect what they'd find in person. We fix that."

## 4. What We Do · 2 minutes

> "We do four things, and most clients use two or three of them together."

Walk through each in ~30 seconds. Tie back to what they told you in step 1.

### 1. Websites
> "Custom, mobile-first, fast. We build in 7 to 14 days depending on the package. You own the domain, the code, everything — even the email. If you ever wanted to leave us, you'd take it all with you."

### 2. Content
> "A beautiful site with weak copy is still a weak site. We write the homepage, the service pages, the menu descriptions, the blog posts — and we tune it for search. Bundled into our website packages or available standalone."

### 3. Apps
> "When a website isn't enough — when you need ordering, loyalty, booking, or an internal tool — we build the app. Local agencies quote fifteen to fifty thousand for this work. We do it for a fraction of that. Two packages: App Standard at $4,999 for ordering or loyalty on iOS and Android, App Premium at $9,999 for full custom with integrations and an admin dashboard. All at honest below-market pricing, all under our Care plans for maintenance."

### 4. Google Business Profile
> "This is the one most owners ignore. Your Google listing drives more local foot traffic than your website ever will. We set it up, optimize it, post weekly, respond to reviews, and send you a monthly report so you can actually see calls, directions, and clicks going up. Setup is free with any website package. Ongoing management is in our Care+ plan."

## 5. Pricing · 90 seconds

Don't apologize for the price. State it plainly.

> "I'll give you the real numbers. Our packages run 30 to 45 percent below what most agencies charge for the same work. No contracts, no hidden fees, no surprise upsells. What you see is what you pay."

### Walk the three tiers:

- **Starter — $499** (was $899): 1-page site, contact form, basic SEO, GBP setup, 7-day delivery. For solo owners with simple needs.
- **Standard — $1,499** (was $2,799): 4 to 6 pages, full SEO, analytics, multilingual support, 30 days of edits. This is what most clients pick.
- **Premium — $2,999** (was $5,499): Up to 12 pages, online ordering or booking integration, content for 8 pages, 90 days of priority support. For restaurants, multi-location businesses, or anyone selling online.

### Then Care:

> "Then there's monthly Care. Two options. Fifty-nine a month covers hosting, security, backups, and an hour of edits. One-forty-nine — Care+ — adds content updates, Google Business management, and monthly SEO reports. Most clients on Standard or Premium end up on Care+ because the maintenance is what keeps the site earning."

### Apps:

> "Apps come in two tiers. $4,999 for App Standard — that covers most clients: ordering, loyalty, or booking, working on iOS and Android, with an admin panel. $9,999 for App Premium — full custom with integrations and a dashboard. Local agencies quote two to five times that. If an app is in scope for you, we'll walk through which tier fits after this call."

> "One thing I'll flag now so it's not a surprise later: Apple and Google charge developer fees to put an app in their stores — Apple's is $99 a year, Google's is $25 one-time. Those go directly from you to Apple and Google. We never mark them up."

## 6. Why Us (vs. The Alternatives) · 60 seconds

If they bring it up, address it head-on. Don't pretend the alternatives don't exist.

> "You've probably got three other options on the table. Let me save you the homework."

- **Wix / Squarespace / a teenager:** Fine for a hobby. Won't rank, won't scale, and you'll outgrow it. Then you pay twice — once to build it, once to redo it.
- **An agency:** Will quote $8k to $25k, take three months, and assign you to an account manager who doesn't know your business. We deliver in two weeks for a fraction of the price.
- **Offshore freelancer:** Cheap. You'll save money. You'll also spend six months chasing edits across time zones, and when something breaks you'll be on your own.

> "We sit in the middle. Agency-quality work, freelancer pricing, founder-level attention. Two-week turnaround. And you own everything."

## Objection Handlers

### "It's too expensive."
> "Compared to what? An agency would charge three times this. A Wix site costs less but won't rank — and you'll pay us in 18 months anyway to redo it. The Starter is $499 and it'll start earning the day it goes live. That's the cheapest version of getting this right."

### "I already have a website."
> "Great — when was the last time it brought you a customer you could trace? If you can't answer that, the site isn't working. Let me audit it for free. If it's fine, I'll tell you. If it's not, I'll tell you what to fix — even if you don't hire me."

### "Can my nephew / cousin / friend build it?"
> "Maybe. The site itself isn't the hard part — anyone can put pages on a screen. The hard part is making it load fast, rank on Google, convert visitors, stay updated, and not break six months from now. If they can do that, hire them. If they can't, you'll be calling me to fix it for twice the price."

### "I need to think about it."
> "Totally fair. Can I ask what specifically you want to think about? Sometimes I can answer it right now."  
(Most "let me think" is really "I'm not sure about X." Drag it into the open.)

### "Can you do it cheaper?"
> "Not on this package. But I can right-size you to a smaller one. Starter at $499 gives you a working presence and Google setup — we can grow from there. I'd rather sell you what you actually need than discount what you don't."

### "What if I want to leave you?"
> "You can. Anytime. The domain is in your name, the code is yours, your accounts are yours. There's no contract. Care plans cancel anytime. We earn the relationship every month — we don't lock you in."

### "Are there any hidden fees?"
> "No markup, no surprises. The only third-party costs that ever come through are pass-throughs at cost: domain renewal (about $29/yr after the first year), and for apps, Apple's $99/yr developer fee and Google's $25 one-time fee. Those go straight from you to the vendor — we never touch them. Everything else is in the package price you see."

### "How fast can you actually deliver?"
> "Starter in 7 days. Standard in 10 days. Premium in 14 days. The clock starts the day you send me your content — photos, business info, hours. If your content is ready, we can launch by next [day of week]."

### "Do you work with people in [their industry]?"
> "Yes — but here's the honest version. If we haven't built for your industry before, I'll tell you. We have built across food, catering, cafes, retail, services, IT. If yours is different, I'll quote a discovery hour up front to make sure we understand the space before we build."

### "Can you do this in Hindi / Urdu / Spanish / Arabic?"
> "Yes. Multilingual is included in Standard and Premium. Not as a Google Translate plugin — actual translated pages, with the right tone for the audience."

### "I'm not sure I need an app."
> "Most businesses don't need an app — they need a great website and a working Google profile. We'll only recommend an app if you have repeat customers, ordering volume, or an operational problem a website can't solve. Let's start with the basics."

## Notes for the presenter

- **Don't talk about technology.** Talk about customers, calls, walk-ins, orders, growth. The tech is the *how*. They care about the *what*.
- **Reference the room.** If you noticed something specific about their business in pre-call prep, mention it. "I saw your Facebook has 2,400 followers — that's an audience your website should be pulling from." Generic pitches lose to specific ones every time.
- **Silence is a tool.** After you give the price, stop talking. Let them respond.
- **Don't badmouth competitors.** Compare honestly. You win on attention, transparency, and ownership — not on trashing other shops.
- **Always offer a no-cost next step.** Free audit, free discovery call, free quote. Lower the friction.
- **Track every call.** Date, name, business, what they said, what you quoted, what they said yes/no to. Patterns show up after 10–15 calls and tell you what to change.
